# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Nuclii serves a pre-launch audience across both sides of a real-world experience marketplace: attendees looking for things happening near them; hosts, society leads, community organisers, service providers, talent, creatives, and venues who need structure for discovery, booking, access, and coordination; plus early contributors, partners, and investors evaluating whether the platform is credible and alive.

Visitors are usually arriving before the full product exists. They need to understand what Nuclii is, see that it is broader than a generic events app, and choose a clear next step: join early access, become an early host, build with the team, or get in touch.

## Product Purpose

Nuclii is a UK-based real-world experience platform and pre-launch website positioned as infrastructure for real-life experiences. The site exists to explain the category, drive early access signups, attract early hosts and communities, recruit early team members and contributors, collect partner and investor interest, and make the product feel active before launch.

Success means visitors quickly understand that Nuclii helps people discover, host, book, and manage real-world experiences without relying on followers, group chats, or social pressure.

## Brand Personality

Nuclii should feel clear, premium, calm, alive, safe, modern, trustworthy, culturally aware, ambitious, and product-led.

The voice is confident but not arrogant, warm but not childish, simple but not basic, and ambitious without hype. It should sound investor-ready and Gen Z / young professional friendly without collapsing into slang or nightlife-only positioning.

## Anti-references

Nuclii must not look or sound like a generic events app, nightlife app, student-only app, social media feed, follower-first platform, influencer-first platform, Eventbrite clone, Instagram clone, TikTok-like feed, or party flyer site.

Avoid fake traction numbers, public financial claims, fake urgency, overpromising, long corporate paragraphs, vanity metrics, public attendee-list framing, and visual systems that are overcrowded, childish, too flashy, or club/nightlife-only.

Do not put Investors, Terms, Privacy, Cookies, or Community Guidelines in the main navigation. They are footer-only surfaces.

## Design Principles

- **Every event starts here.** The core line should anchor the Home hero and the broader promise: Nuclii is the starting layer for real-world discovery, hosting, booking, access, and coordination.
- **Infrastructure, not a feed.** Interfaces should feel like a structured real-life operating layer, not an endless social stream or popularity contest.
- **Map-first, privacy-first.** Discovery should feel spatial and alive, while public identity, attendee visibility, location reveal, and access should communicate restraint and safety.
- **Many roles, one ecosystem.** Attendees, hosts, societies, service providers, talent, venues, partners, investors, and contributors should all feel included without turning the site into a directory of disconnected audiences.
- **Premium energy, not noise.** Motion, media, and blue accents should make the brand feel alive while keeping conversion paths calm and clear.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA as the operating accessibility bar for public marketing pages. Preserve semantic HTML, visible focus states, labelled controls, useful alt text, keyboard navigation, readable contrast, and reduced-motion alternatives.

Early access forms must stay short and purposeful, include 16+ confirmation where the flow collects launch interest, include marketing consent for updates, and avoid collecting unnecessary personal information.
