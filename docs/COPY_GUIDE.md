# COPY_GUIDE.md

## Copy Principles

Nuclii copy should be:

- clear
- premium
- direct
- warm
- confident
- easy to understand
- investor-ready
- Gen Z / young professional friendly

Avoid:

- overhype
- cliché startup language
- long corporate wording
- excessive slang
- “revolutionary” language
- fake urgency
- sounding like a nightlife app only
- sounding like a social media app

Use short sections and strong CTAs.

---

## Main Product Explanation

Use this as the primary explanation:

> Nuclii helps people discover, host, book, and manage real-world experiences without relying on followers, group chats, or social pressure.

Alternative shorter version:

> Nuclii is infrastructure for discovering, hosting, and accessing real-world experiences.

Expanded version:

> Nuclii brings attendees, hosts, communities, service providers, talent, and venues into one platform built for real-life experiences.

> Build events, workshops, pickups, appointments, private gatherings, and pop-ups from one flow.

> Choose whether locations are public, area-only, revealed after booking, or revealed close to the time.

> Run an interest check before committing fully.

## FAQ Copy

### What is Nuclii?

> Nuclii is a platform for discovering, hosting, booking, and managing real-world experiences.

### Is Nuclii only for students?

> No. Students may be part of Nuclii’s early community, but Nuclii is not a student-only platform.

### Is Nuclii only for events?

> No. Nuclii can support events, workshops, pickup windows, appointment slots, pop-ups, private gatherings, talent showcases, and venue-led experiences.

### When is Nuclii launching?

> Nuclii is currently in pre-launch. Join Early Access to be considered for beta updates.

### Can I host on Nuclii?

> Yes. Early hosts can register interest through the Early Access page.

### Can venues join?

> Yes. Venues can register interest to connect with hosts, communities, and future opportunities.

### Will my real name be public?

> Nuclii is being designed with privacy-first principles. Public identity and attendance visibility will be handled carefully.

## Legal Page Tone

Legal pages should be:

- readable
- clear
- simple
- not overly casual
- not full of unnecessary legal jargon

Use “Last updated” at the top.

Include a note that full app terms may be updated before the public launch.
